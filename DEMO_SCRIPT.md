# Opalite Demo Script

**Total runtime: 2-3 minutes**
**Location: Park near Dublin, CA (lookout area with SF view)**
**Equipment: Phone on lanyard or held chest-height, earbuds in**

---

## INTRO (15 sec)
- Title card: Opalite logo + "See through crafted light."
- Text overlay: "2.2 billion people worldwide have a vision impairment. — WHO"
- Cut to: phone screen showing Opalite landing page → tap Start

---

## SCENE 1: "The Path" — Walking Navigation (60 sec)

**Setup:** Start on a park trail. Hold phone pointing ahead at chest height.

**What to do:** Just walk. Let the AI describe what's ahead naturally via voice conversation.

**Talk to the app:**
1. "What's ahead of me?"
   - AI describes the path, trees, any obstacles
2. Walk toward a bench or sign
   - "What does that sign say?" (point camera at a park sign)
   - AI reads the text
3. Approach stairs or a slope
   - "Is it safe to walk forward?"
   - AI describes the terrain

**Why this works for judges:** Shows real-time navigation, text reading, safety awareness. A blind person could actually use this to walk through a park.

---

## SCENE 2: "The View" — Scene Understanding (60 sec)

**Setup:** Walk to the SF lookout point. Point phone at the cityscape.

**Talk to the app:**
1. "Describe what you see."
   - AI describes the city skyline, water, bridges, buildings
2. "Can you see the Golden Gate Bridge?"
   - AI responds based on what's actually visible
3. "What's the weather look like from here?"
   - AI describes sky conditions, visibility
4. Pan to something closer — a tree, a fence, another person
   - "Who's near me?"
   - AI describes

**Why this works for judges:** Shows the emotional side — a blind person experiencing a view they've never "seen." Powerful moment. Also demonstrates scene-level understanding vs just object detection.

## FILMING TIPS
- **Use voice, NOT tapping** — voice interaction works reliably
- **Earbuds in** — so your phone mic picks up your voice clearly without speaker feedback
- **Walk slowly** — gives the AI time to process at 1 FPS
- **Natural light** — better image quality = better AI descriptions
- **Film in landscape** — easier to edit, looks better on Devpost
- **Record phone screen simultaneously** — use Android built-in screen recorder so judges can see the UI
- **B-roll idea:** film yourself from a distance walking with the phone, then cut to the phone's POV

## WHAT TO AVOID
- Don't show the tapping feature (buggy)
- Don't ask it to describe things it can't see (behind you, too far)
- Don't rapid-fire questions — let it finish responding
- Don't walk too fast — 1 FPS camera needs time
